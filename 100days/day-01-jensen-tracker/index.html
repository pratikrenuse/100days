01
One billion robots by 2035
Not a niche market. His words: the next multi-trillion-dollar industry. He has already named the winner, Tesla's Optimus, and is building the training infrastructure that makes it possible. One billion humanoid robots walking the earth within a decade. He is not speculating. He is building the factory for them right now.
02
Data centers are the new oil refineries
He stopped calling them data centers. Now they are AI factories. "Just like electricity needs power plants, AI needs factories," he said at Computex 2025. Countries and companies still thinking of compute as IT infrastructure are already two moves behind. The ones buying are South Korea, Saudi Arabia, and every serious nation on earth.
03
A millionfold more power in ten years
He told an industry conference that computing power is growing fourfold every single year. Compound that for a decade and you get a millionfold increase. Every AI model running today will look like a pocket calculator by 2035. He is not saying this to sound impressive. He is saying it to explain why he is building what he is building.
04
The next bottleneck is not chips. It is electricity.
On Joe Rogan he said it plainly: energy, not GPUs, is now the limit for AI. He predicts small nuclear reactors sitting next to data centers within six years. Goldman Sachs compared the coming energy surge to adding a brand new top-10 energy-consuming country to the global grid overnight. China has twice the energy capacity and builds data centers in months. The US takes three years. Jensen has already said who wins that race if nothing changes. Follow the power lines, not the chip stocks.
